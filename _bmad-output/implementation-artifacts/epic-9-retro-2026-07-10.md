## 2. What Went Well (ratified by Borja)

1. **The correct-course of 9.1** — detecting the author-name data gap during the 9.1 review and spinning it out to 9.4/9.5 instead of inflating the review's scope. Textbook discipline: found the gap, documented it as D9, homed it in its own story pair.
2. **The 9.4 → 9.5 → (9.2, 9.3) sequence** — data first, contract second, render and e2e last. The binding dependency (Top-5 render needs 9.5 landed) was managed without blockers.
3. **AD-12 (RBAC in-SQL) held end-to-end** — every aggregation filters by `allowedChannelIds` *inside* the SQL, verified at source in all 5 reviews. No KPI, bar, or donut leaks private-channel volume.
4. **Lean delivery** — the entire view is flex/grid + CSS gradients; 1 new column + 1 index; zero chart dependencies; no new table.
5. **Zero production incidents** — consistent with the whole 9-epic arc; all issues caught in test/review.

---

## 3. What Didn't Go Well / Friction

1. **Scope moved during execution (Borja's stated biggest pain)** — Epic 9 started as 3 stories and finished as 5. The same correct-course mechanism that spun out 9.1's gap cleanly also meant the plan "breathed" mid-flight. Compounded at the epic level: **both** Epic 8 and Epic 9 are post-roadmap, correct-course-born additions. The team has been operating in "continuous addition" mode without a stable planning container since the roadmap completed at Epic 7.
2. **Blind Hunter severity noise (recurring, still open from Epic 7)** — inflated-severity false positives from the Blind Hunter in reviews of 9.2, 9.3, and 9.4, all refuted against source. The Acceptance Auditor was clean every time; the Blind consistently over-flagged. This is the **3rd** appearance of the pattern (Epic 7 AI → 9.2/9.3/9.4) and the action item remains open.

---

## 4. Key Insights

- The correct-course tool is dual-natured: a strength when it prevents review scope-creep, a cost when it destabilizes the epic plan. The fix is not to stop using it — it's to make the *mode* explicit (see §7 significant reorientation).
- The product is **functionally complete but has never run in a real production environment.** All validation was via the e2e harness and the local docker stack.
- Statistics view has **no remaining UX debt** — 9.3 asserted every visual AC via `getComputedStyle`. The next effort is infrastructure, not screens.
